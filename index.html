How This Works

This is a group project for four people. You decide together who does what — but every member must sign off on every section before submission. If it is in the folder, all four of you are standing behind it.

Amazing Body Shapes

🦴 Cube-like shapes in your skull

The cranium is assembled from flat-sided bones that fit together at fixed joints called sutures.

  • Research the Parietal bones — where they sit and what they look like.
  • Notice how flat faces fit against neighbouring bones with no gaps.
  • Why might flat-faced shapes be better for protection than rounded ones?
📋Portfolio response: Why must skull bones fit with no gaps? What would happen if they didn't? Use at least one geometry word.

🦷 Pyramid & prism shapes in your mouth

  • Canine teeth — pointed like a triangular pyramid. Built to pierce and tear.
  • Molars — broad like a rectangular prism. Built to grind.
  • Which concentrates force at a point? Which spreads force over a surface?
  • Which is better for biting a mango vs chewing rice?
📋Portfolio response: How does each tooth shape help you eat different foods? Use: pointy, flat, force, surface area.

Tessellations — Nature's Own Geometry

A tessellation is a pattern of shapes covering a flat surface with no gaps and no overlaps. You will create two.

Materials for both: 2 pieces of Letter-sized paper (different colours) · tracing paper · pencil · ruler · scissors · glue stick

Tessellation 1 — Geometric Mix

Choose one:

  • Triangles (4 cm sides) + hexagons, OR
  • Triangles (4 cm sides) + squares

Fill the whole page, no gaps, at least 4 repeats, neatly coloured.

📋Portfolio: Finished tessellation + your group's definition of a tessellation in your own words + why there must be no gaps.

Tessellation 2 — Your Own Nature Pattern

Find a pattern in nature — any plant, animal, or surface — that tessellates. Do not copy another group's pattern.

  • Fill the whole page, no gaps.
  • Repeat at least 6 times.
  • Colour to match your nature inspiration.
📋Portfolio: Name your pattern, describe it in real life, explain why it appears in nature, and give two tessellation examples outside of nature with the shape used in each.
